Portrait of Us

Campaign for Urban Sophistication — lockdown edition

A visual narrative exploring connection during isolation. When Urban Sophistication approached me during lockdown with the challenge of bringing together 100 fans in a single image, we embarked on a journey that would evolve from a crowdsourced collage into something more resonant and intimate — a meditation on presence and absence in digital space.

The Initial Approach

During the height of lockdown, Urban Sophistication wanted to create something that would connect their community while acknowledging our collective isolation. We began with a democratic approach: inviting fans to contribute self-portraits taken at home, guided by a set of visual instructions I developed to create cohesion across varied environments.

Reconsidering the Narrative

As dozens of portraits arrived—raw, intimate glimpses into private spaces—I began composing them into a single image. Yet both the client and I felt something was missing. The crowd of faces created visual noise rather than connection, and the emotional weight of isolation was being lost in the density of the composition.

Shifting Direction

We pivoted, choosing to work with a smaller group of models who could represent the collection while allowing for more control over the visual language. In a studio setting, I directed these subjects—perfecting lighting, framing, and expression to capture the emotional tone we were seeking.

Building the Space Between

From these anchoring portraits, I began constructing a digital environment—not a literal space, but a visual metaphor for our collective experience. Through 3D modeling and careful composition, I created a room filled with screens, each displaying fragments of lives from the original submissions.

The Final Composition

The room took shape: studio models existed in a constructed reality, surrounded by screens showing the community's submitted portraits—silent, glowing presences that spoke to our digital connectedness during physical separation. This layered approach allowed us to tell a more nuanced story about isolation and the new ways we were learning to be together.

Technical Process

The final images required intensive layering: combining studio photography, 3D environments, screen compositing, and careful lighting adjustment to create a cohesive whole. Each element was manipulated independently, allowing precise control over the visual narrative and emotional impact.

Impact & Publication

The resulting series formed the centerpiece of Urban Sophistication's lockdown campaign, appearing in Women's Wear Daily and garnering significant engagement across social platforms. More importantly, it provided the community with a visual language for understanding our shared experience—acknowledging separation while celebrating connection.

"What began as a simple idea to feature our community evolved into something far more profound. The final images captured exactly what we were feeling but couldn't articulate—that strange mixture of isolation and togetherness that defined this period. Working with this level of creative vision transformed our campaign."

— Urban Sophistication Team
