First Semester Project

A first architectural study at the Royal Danish Academy, developed through folded models, timber logic, collage and uncertainty.

The brief asked for a room for hygiene. I approached it as a bathhouse: intimate, partly concealed, and held between structure and atmosphere.

I began with folded cardboard models and translated their geometries into timber frames. The translation was not exact: the structural logic of the timber changed the original forms, and these differences became part of the investigation.

Two of the frames were then brought together and explored through axonometric drawing. Rather than resolving their mismatch immediately, I worked with the overlaps, gaps and awkward transitions to discover new spatial possibilities.

I continued the investigation through a 1:25 model. I treated it as a working tool rather than a representation of a finished design, rebuilding and adjusting parts as new relationships appeared.

The project gradually developed into a bathhouse with an intimate and uncertain atmosphere. Blurred figures appear behind glass-block walls, while a stair rises towards a large thatched roof. The upper space remains partly concealed, suggesting something beyond what can be seen from below..

Throughout the project, I moved between models, drawings and collage, using each medium to examine the work differently. At the final review, the most meaningful feedback was that I had stayed with the complexity of the project rather than resolving it too early. Some questions remained open, but this willingness to remain inside the uncertainty became one of the project’s strengths.

Open Discussion
